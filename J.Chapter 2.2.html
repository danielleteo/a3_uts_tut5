chapter 2

writing on the wall

Best to keep a clear head. He should be here anytime now.

The straggle of patrons gives him a clear view of his expected guest, though he still jumps a little at the large hand that claps his shoulder from behind.

"Now, Jaz, not playing dirty, are we?" The words are followed by a self-indulgent laugh. Avon rounds the table, flicking a dismissive hand towards the dealer, before dropping into the seat next to Jaz with a thump.

He looks a right crook, Jaz thinks, rolling his eyes and fixing a glare at the man's smug grin. The light, dirty-blond stubble spattered across his chin, along with the unsettling glint in his murky green eyes, paints the poster image of a conman.

The scowl on Jaz's face, however, only widens the taunting smile.

"It is still Jaz, isn't it?" he continues when Jaz gives no response, "With the one 'z'?"

Jaz considers responding with his hands, though the crude reminder that his lean physique doesn’t prove much of an advantage stops him short.

The casino head takes the silence as an answer in the affirmative. "Hah, figures."

Jaz's tongue fires back before he can think to restrain it. "It's still Avon, right? A for Asshole?"

Avon narrows his eyes, dull in comparison to the acerbic emerald of Jaz's.

"Watch yourself," he hisses. Jaz rolls his eyes but resigns any further jabs.

"Why send your man to pay us a visit? If you wanted to see me, you could just call." The sarcasm and underlying threat drip off the sentence, but Avon only gives a dry chuckle.

"Don't flatter yourself." He shoots Jaz a pointed look, then directs his gaze not-so-subtly to the watch on his wrist. "If we had any business, you would know. I don't send lackeys to collect my dues."


"Who else would it be?"
